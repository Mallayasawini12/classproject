Help & Documentation

Everything you need to know about using EmotiSense

Quick Start Guide

Step 1: Allow Camera Access

When you first visit the Live Feed page, your browser will ask for permission to access your camera. Click "Allow" to enable emotion detection.

Step 2: Position Yourself

Make sure your face is clearly visible and well-lit. The system works best when your face is centered within the camera view.

Step 3: Watch the Detection

The system will automatically detect your facial expressions and display the dominant emotion in real-time on the video feed.

Step 4: View Analytics

Click "View Analytics Dashboard" to see detailed statistics, charts, and emotion breakdowns with percentages.

Step 5: Export Data

From the dashboard, you can export your emotion data in CSV or JSON format for further analysis.

Frequently Asked Questions

What emotions can EmotiSense detect?

EmotiSense can detect 7 different emotions: Happy, Cry (Sad), Angry, Surprise, Fear, Disgust, and Neutral. Each emotion is analyzed based on facial features and expressions.

How accurate is the emotion detection?

EmotiSense uses state-of-the-art deep learning models (DeepFace) which achieve high accuracy rates (typically 90-98%). Accuracy depends on lighting conditions, face visibility, and expression clarity.

Is my video data stored or shared?

No, EmotiSense processes all video data locally in your browser and on the server in real-time. No video recordings are saved, and only emotion statistics are temporarily stored during your session.

Can I use EmotiSense on mobile devices?

Yes! EmotiSense is fully responsive and works on mobile devices, tablets, and desktops. Make sure your device has a front-facing camera and grant camera permissions in your browser.

How do I reset the statistics?

On the Analytics Dashboard, click the "Reset Statistics" button to clear all emotion data and start a new session. You can also use the API endpoint /api/reset with a POST request.

What file formats can I export data to?

You can export your emotion data in two formats: CSV (for spreadsheet applications like Excel) and JSON (for programmatic access and data analysis tools).

Why is "No face detected" showing?

This message appears when the system cannot detect a face in the video frame. Ensure you're in a well-lit area, facing the camera directly, and that your entire face is visible within the frame.

Can multiple people use EmotiSense simultaneously?

Currently, EmotiSense is optimized for single-user detection. While multiple faces may be detected, the system focuses on the most prominent face in the video frame.

API Documentation

Available API Endpoints

GET /api/emotions

Get current emotion statistics and session information

GET /api/history

Retrieve emotion history with timestamps (last 50 entries)

POST /api/reset

Reset all emotion statistics and start a new session

GET /api/export/json

Download emotion data as JSON file

GET /api/export/csv

Download emotion data as CSV file

Troubleshooting

Camera Not Working

Check browser permissions, ensure no other application is using the camera, and try refreshing the page.

Low Accuracy

Improve lighting conditions, ensure your face is fully visible, and avoid extreme angles or distances.

Page Not Loading

Clear browser cache, check internet connection, and ensure the server is running properly.
